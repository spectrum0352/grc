
7 Strategies to Stay Confident Under Pressure
1. Know Your Triggers
Recognizing the specific patterns that spark your anxiety can prevent emotional spirals2.
	• Actionable Step: Journal your stress responses to identify trends and reframe those reactions with supportive actions3.
	• Example Scenario: If you notice that "last-minute status updates" cause your heart to race, you can reframe this by having a pre-written template ready to go, turning a chaotic request into a routine task.
2. Power Up with Body Language
Using your physical presence can help manage internal anxiety4.
	• Actionable Step: Practice power poses in a mirror daily and use video recordings to refine your nonverbal cues5.
	• Example Scenario: Before a major presentation, spend two minutes in a "Wonder Woman" or "Superman" pose (hands on hips, feet wide). This physical openness can help you feel more assertive before you even speak.
3. Prepare Like a Pro
Thorough preparation cuts through uncertainty and allows confidence to thrive6.
	• Actionable Step: Brainstorm potential obstacles and run mock trials to solidify your readiness7.
	• Example Scenario: If you are heading into a difficult negotiation, brainstorm the three toughest questions you might be asked. Practice your answers out loud until they feel natural.
4. Visualize Success
Guided imagery helps rewire the brain to expect victory8.
	• Actionable Step: Dedicate 10 minutes a day to visualization or write vivid descriptions of best-case outcomes9.
	• Example Scenario: Instead of worrying about tripping on stage, spend time visualizing yourself walking calmly to the podium, seeing the audience nodding in agreement, and hearing the applause at the end.
5. Play to Your Strengths
Shift your focus away from your flaws and toward what makes you unique10.
	• Actionable Step: Complete a strengths assessment and develop a "power statement" summarizing your key skills11.
	• Example Scenario: If you aren't the best at public speaking but excel at data analysis, lean into your strength by making your visuals the "star" of the show, allowing your expertise to shine through the data.
6. Pause Before Reacting
Thoughtful responses exude control, whereas immediate reactions can seem impulsive12.
	• Actionable Step: Practice a 3-second pause before replying and use deep breaths to steady your nerves13.

	• Example Scenario: If a colleague criticizes your work in a meeting, take a slow breath and count to three. This prevents a defensive snap-back and allows you to respond with, "I appreciate that perspective; let's look at the data together."
7. Celebrate Past Wins
Your previous achievements serve as fuel and build resilience for the future15.
	• Actionable Step: Keep a "success journal" to revisit your wins and share proud stories with trusted allies16.
	• Example Scenario: When facing a daunting new project, look back at your success journal. Reminding yourself, "I successfully launched the 2024 campaign under a tighter deadline than this," provides the evidence you need to trust your current capabilities.
